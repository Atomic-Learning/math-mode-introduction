Math mode is a way of writing plain-text characters, that will later be rendered into mathematical expressions in a document. It is used in many different contexts, including LaTeX, Markdown, and HTML. In each context, it will have different syntax and rules for how to denote a piece of text as math mode content, but the core syntax of the math mode content itself is the same.

Math mode is very flexible and allows for a wide range of mathematical expressions to be written. The (physically meaningless) example below was created using mathmode syntax:

$$
 \frac{\partial^2 \overrightarrow{x}}{\partial t^2} = \begin{pmatrix}
\alpha  & \frac{1}{2} \\
\theta  & \rho ^{2} \\
\end{pmatrix}
$$
